Travel Expense Eligibility

Explains which engineering travel costs are reimbursable for supplier visits, test tracks, and launch support.

Policy Explanation

Travel Expense Eligibility should read like an answer from HR to an employee who wants to know what happens next. This page explains which engineering travel costs are reimbursable for supplier visits, test tracks, and launch support.

The page should explain the rule in plain language first, then point to Expense Tool for the actual request. Managers can use it to understand timing and approval responsibility without copying private employee data into shared notes.

Employee Path

The employee normally starts with travel request and checks whether expense receipt is needed. If the case depends on a date, contract type, country, or release freeze, the manager should ask People Operations before promising an exception.

When the request is complete, the final answer should be recorded in Expense Tool or the approved case system. The shared page stays general so future employees can reuse it.

What HR Checks

  • Owner team is People Operations and has accepted the request or review.
  • Required artifacts are available: travel request, expense receipt, cost center.
  • Relevant systems are checked: Expense Tool, Finance Approval, HR Portal.
  • The final decision or completion note is attached to the ticket, request, or record.

Privacy Note

Keep employee-specific information in Expense Tool or the approved HR case tool. Notes should explain the rule and the expected path, not store private employee details.

Exceptions

Escalate unclear ownership, missing evidence, blocked approvals, or exceptions to People Operations. The usual requester is the employee or manager, and the standard service desk or program channel should hold the final note.

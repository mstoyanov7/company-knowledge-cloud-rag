Wellness Budget Claim

Defines eligible wellness expenses and claim evidence for fitness, mental health, and preventive care.

Employee Guide

Wellness Budget Claim is written for an employee comparing benefit options or trying to understand an enrollment step. This page defines eligible wellness expenses and claim evidence for fitness, mental health, and preventive care.

The language should stay practical: what the employee can do, what the company provides, and what needs to happen before a deadline. Detailed personal choices belong in Benefits Portal, not in shared documentation.

How Enrollment Usually Works

The usual path starts by checking eligibility, then opening Benefits Portal and confirming whether claim receipt or wellness category is required. Some benefits need manager or HR confirmation, while others are employee self-service.

When enrollment is complete, the employee should keep the confirmation from Expense Tool or HR Portal. The shared page should explain the process, not store individual benefit selections.

Important Detail

Eligibility can depend on country, contract type, start date, and enrollment window. The page should point employees to Benefits Portal for the actual submission and use Expense Tool or HR Portal only when the benefit owner asks for it.

Completion Checks

  • Owner team is Benefits Team and has accepted the request or review.
  • Required artifacts are available: claim receipt, wellness category, annual limit.
  • Relevant systems are checked: Benefits Portal, Expense Tool, HR Portal.
  • The final decision or completion note is attached to the ticket, request, or record.

Questions

Escalate unclear ownership, missing evidence, blocked approvals, or exceptions to Benefits Team. The usual requester is the employee, and the standard service desk or program channel should hold the final note.

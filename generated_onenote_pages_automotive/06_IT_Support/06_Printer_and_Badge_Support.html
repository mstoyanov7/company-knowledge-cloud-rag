Service Desk Note

Printer and Badge Support is a service desk note for support cases that need a repeatable path. This page documents support flow for office printers, lab badges, site access cards, and visitor badge issues.

The support agent should identify the requester, affected asset or account, business reason, and urgency before changing anything. This is especially important when the request touches engineering tools, test benches, VPN, MFA, or production-adjacent systems.

Support Ownership

Owner teamWorkplace Services
Audienceemployees, service desk agents, endpoint engineers
Review rhythmReview when access rules, tooling, or ownership changes.
Primary systemsBadge System, Service Desk, Facilities Portal

Triage

Start with badge ID and verify it against Badge System. Then check site location and access group so the ticket has enough detail for another agent to continue the work if the first responder is unavailable.

Once the fix is applied, tell the requester exactly what changed and what they should try next. The ticket should not close until the requester confirms the result or the support window expires.

Details To Collect

  • Badge ID maintained by Workplace Services.
  • Site location available before work starts.
  • Access group linked from the ticket, request, or workspace.
  • Requester identity, asset or account ID, business reason, and expected resolution date.

Ticket Closure Checks

  • Owner team is Workplace Services and has accepted the request or review.
  • Required artifacts are available: badge ID, site location, access group.
  • Relevant systems are checked: Badge System, Service Desk, Facilities Portal.
  • The final decision or completion note is attached to the ticket, request, or record.

Requester Communication

Tell the requester what changed, what still needs their action, and where the ticket is recorded. For this page, the normal systems are Badge System, Service Desk, Facilities Portal.

Support Risks

  • Identity verification is required before access, MFA, or device state changes.
  • Temporary exceptions need expiry dates and owners.
  • Tickets should not be closed until the requester confirms the fix.
